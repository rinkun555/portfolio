How might we design an offboarding process for recently discharged patients?

*Offboarding refers to the process of transitioning patients from hospital care back to their normal lives with proper support and resources.

TIMELINE

March 2023 - June 2023 (3 months)

TEAM

Collaboration with Køge Hospital's oncology department, including 3 healthcare professionals and 2 design researchers.

MY ROLE

Lead UX Researcher and Service Designer responsible for:

  • Conducting interviews with cancer survivors and healthcare staff
  • Creating patient journey maps
  • Facilitating co-design workshops
  • Developing service prototypes

CHALLENGE

Cancer patients often feel abandoned after completing their treatment. The transition from being closely monitored in a hospital setting to suddenly managing on their own creates anxiety and uncertainty. Healthcare providers needed a structured way to support patients during this critical transition period.

"The day after my last treatment, I felt like I was standing on the edge of a cliff. The hospital had been my safety net for months, and suddenly it was gone."

SOLUTION

We designed a comprehensive offboarding process that includes:

  • A personalized transition plan created with each patient
  • Digital follow-up system with scheduled check-ins
  • Peer support network connecting current patients with survivors
  • Resource toolkit for managing common post-treatment challenges

TAKE AWAY

The project highlighted the importance of designing healthcare services that extend beyond clinical treatment. By applying co-design methodologies with both patients and healthcare providers, we created solutions that address emotional and practical needs during life transitions.

METHODS & TOOLS

  • Semi-structured interviews
  • Journey mapping
  • Co-design workshops
  • Service blueprinting
  • Prototype testing
  • Qualitative data analysis
