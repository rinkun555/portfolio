Facilitating Online Co-Design Workshop

TEAM

Diana Vleju, Samira Aliverdipour, Yichun Lin, Rio Suzuki

Background

Facilitated an online co-design workshop with key stakeholders. Although we initially aimed for a physical session, it was not feasible due to the varied and busy schedules of the participants. However, because our goal was to enable systemic change, we challenged ourselves to find a solution. Ultimately, we successfully organized and facilitated an online co-design session that brought together the voices we needed.

MY ROLE

  • ・Organizer and Communication Lead
  • ・Enabling Iterative Co-Design
  • ・Framework Strategist and Question Designer
  • ・Insight-Driven Listener and Responsive Interviewer

APPROACH

TAKE AWAY

In-Depth Preparation

The quality of preparation shapes the quality of outcomes. While co-design requires space for participant input and spontaneity, focused and intensive research beforehand is essential. It allows us to follow up meaningfully, connect the dots in real time, and steer the session toward valuable insights

Adaptability Through Empathy

Co-design is a relational process that involves negotiation between diverse stakeholders. To adapt effectively, we must step into their perspectives, understand their motivations, and design interactions that reflect their realities.

METHODS & TOOLS

  • Desk Research
  • Semi Structured Interviews
  • Observation
  • Co-Design
  • System Mapping

FINAL DELIVERABLES

  • System Maps
